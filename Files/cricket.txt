On a field of green beneath the sun,
Where battles are fought but wars are none,
Where leather meets willow with thunder’s sound,
And hopes of millions gather around.

The crowd awakens with a rising cheer,
As the players walk with courage clear,
White lines painted on sacred ground,
Where dreams and legends both are found.

The bowler runs with fiery pace,
Determination on his face,
Each step echoes across the field,
A test of skill no heart can shield.

The batsman waits, calm and still,
Eyes like steel, mind of will,
Bat raised high in silent grace,
Ready to meet the ball’s fierce race.

The ball flies fast through air so tight,
A flash of red, a streak of light,
The bat swings bold, the crowd takes breath,
In that moment—life or death.

A crack! The sound that fans adore,
The ball speeds swiftly toward the shore,
Across the grass it runs away,
Four bright cheers begin to play.

But cricket is more than runs and fame,
More than victory, more than game,
It’s patience carved in every hour,
A quiet test of mind and power.

A fielder dives with fearless heart,
A moment’s chance to play his part,
The ball held tight within his hand,
A hero rising from the sand.

The wicket falls, the stumps fly high,
A roar explodes into the sky,
The bowler smiles with pride so wide,
His teammates gather by his side.

The sun moves slow across the day,
Shadows stretch along the clay,
Each over tells a different tale,
Of courage strong or spirits frail.

Some innings bloom like summer rain,
Others fade in silent pain,
But every player knows the art—
Cricket begins within the heart.

From dusty streets where children play,
With broken bats in joyful sway,
To grand arenas filled with light,
The same passion burns bright.

The young boy dreams with hopeful eyes,
Of lifting cups toward cheering skies,
Of centuries carved in history’s page,
And thunderous applause upon the stage.

The captain stands with thoughtful mind,
A leader calm yet fierce in kind,
Setting fields like pieces on a board,
Every move a silent sword.

Day turns to dusk, the match grows tight,
Every run now sparks a fight,
The final over draws so near,
Hearts beat fast with hope and fear.

Six runs needed, one ball to play,
The bowler prays, the batsman sways,
The crowd stands still in breathless pause,
As destiny rewrites its laws.

The ball is bowled with desperate might,
The bat connects in perfect flight,
High it climbs into the air,
A soaring arc beyond despair.

Over the rope the ball descends—
A six! The stadium never ends,
Fireworks burst, the victory song,
A moment fans remember long.

For cricket lives in joy and pain,
In sunlit glory, storm and rain,
In every child who dares to dream,
And every fan within the stream.

It’s more than sport, more than play,
A story told in every day,
Of patience, pride, and spirits strong,
Where every heart can still belong.

So when the bat and ball appear,
And stadium echoes fill the air,
Remember this simple truth we see—

Cricket is not just a game…
It is a lifelong melody. 🏏
